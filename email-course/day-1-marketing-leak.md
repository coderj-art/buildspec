# Day 1: The Marketing Leak

**Subject:** you're losing 20 hours a week
**Sub-text:** and you probably don't even realise where it's going

---

You know that feeling where you sit down on Monday, open your laptop, and think "right, I need to post something"?

Then you spend 45 minutes trying to figure out what to say.

Then you write it. Rewrite it. Post it.

Then you do the same thing for your emails. Your stories. Your newsletter. Your whatever-else-you're-supposed-to-be-doing.

And before you know it, it's Wednesday afternoon and you've spent 12 hours on marketing. And you still feel behind.

That's the marketing leak.

**The Mistake**

Doing your own marketing manually when 80% of it could run itself.

Most business owners I talk to are spending 20+ hours a week on marketing tasks. Content creation. Email writing. Social scheduling. Responding to DMs. Writing captions.

And here's the thing. They're good at it. That's not the problem.

The problem is they're doing work that should be done once and repeated. Instead, they start from scratch every single time.

Every Monday. Blank screen. "What should I post this week?"

Every Thursday. Blank email draft. "What should I send my list?"

Same cycle. Every week. For months.

**The Reason This Happens**

You started as a one-person show.

Marketing was just "post stuff." There was no system. No process. You just did whatever felt right that day.

And it worked. For a while.

But then the business grew. More platforms. More content types. More people expecting to hear from you. And you never built a system underneath it. You just kept doing more.

So now you're spending half your working week on marketing. And the other half wishing you had more time for the actual work.

I did the same thing for months. I was writing every email from scratch. Recording every video without a plan. Posting on three platforms and doing well on none of them.

It wasn't until I stopped and asked "what am I actually doing every week that I've already done before?" that things changed.

The answer was almost everything.

The problem isn't that you're bad at marketing. The problem is you're doing it like it's 2019. Manually. One piece at a time. From scratch.

**How To Fix It**

Three things. You can do all of these today.

**1. Pick your one channel.**

Not three. Not five. One.

The one that actually brings you customers. For most people, that's email or one social platform. Everything else is noise right now.

I know it feels like you need to be everywhere. You don't. You need to be good somewhere. Pick the channel that's already working, even a little bit, and go all in on that one.

Cut the rest. You can always add them back later when you have a system.

**2. Build one automated sequence.**

Take your best-performing content from the last 3 months. The emails people replied to. The posts that got traction. The stuff that actually resonated.

Turn it into a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers. Write it once. Set it to run automatically. Every new lead gets your best stuff without you lifting a finger.

This one change means your marketing works while you sleep. Not all of it. But enough that Monday mornings feel different.

**3. Let AI handle first drafts.**

Stop staring at blank screens. Give AI your topic, your audience, and one example of how you write. Let it produce the first draft. You edit.

This alone cuts content time by 60%. Not because the AI writes better than you. Because the blank screen is what kills you. Starting from something is always faster than starting from nothing.

That's it. One channel. One sequence. AI first drafts.

You don't need to overhaul your entire marketing. You just need to stop starting from scratch every single day.
