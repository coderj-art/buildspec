# Day 5: The Focus Leak

**Subject:** the leak that ties them all together
**Sub-text:** this is the one nobody talks about

---

Yesterday we talked about the content leak. Paying for AI tools and still doing hours of content work because the system was missing.

Today's the last leak. And it's different from the other four.

Because this one isn't about marketing. Or operations. Or support. Or content.

It's about you.

Think about yesterday. How many times did you switch roles?

You started the morning answering emails. Then you jumped into a client task. Then you checked your socials. Then you replied to a support message. Then you went back to the client task. Then you wrote a caption. Then you did some bookkeeping. Then you...

You get it.

That's the focus leak.

**The Mistake**

Context switching between 6 roles every day and calling it "hustle."

You're the CEO. The marketer. The support team. The content creator. The admin. The bookkeeper. Sometimes the cleaner.

And every single time you switch from one role to another, you lose focus. A study from UC Irvine found each switch costs about 23 minutes to fully re-engage. Not because the switch itself takes that long. But because your brain needs that time to get back into the thing you were doing before.

If you switch roles 8 times a day, that's 2 hours lost. Just from switching. Not from doing anything wrong. Not from being lazy. Just from the way the human brain works.

Two hours a day. Ten hours a week. Forty hours a month. Gone. On nothing.

**The Reason This Happens**

You wear every hat. So you react to whatever's in front of you.

A customer emails at 9am. You reply. A DM comes in at 9:15. You answer. Your project management tool pings at 9:30. You check it. Your email list software sends a notification at 9:45. You look.

By 10am you've touched four different systems, handled four different types of work, and haven't actually made progress on anything.

This isn't a discipline problem. It's a structure problem.

You don't have defined times for defined work. So everything bleeds into everything else. And you end the day feeling exhausted but unable to point to what you actually accomplished.

The other four leaks we've covered this week. Marketing, operations, support, content. They all feed this one. Because when none of those systems are automated, YOU are the system. And the system can't focus because it's being pulled in five directions at once.

**How To Fix It**

**1. Block your day into 3 zones.**

Morning: deep work. The one thing that moves your business forward. Client work. Product building. Strategy. Whatever your highest-value task is. Do it first. No email. No notifications. No switching.

Middle: communication. Emails. DMs. Support questions. Calls. Batch them all into one block. Respond to everything at once instead of dripping responses throughout the day.

End: admin. Bookkeeping. Invoicing. Planning tomorrow. The stuff that has to happen but shouldn't interrupt your actual work.

Three zones. Three switches. Instead of twenty.

**2. Batch similar tasks.**

Don't write one email, then switch to support, then switch back to writing. Write all your emails at once. Handle all support at once. Do all your admin at once.

Same work. But grouped. Your brain stays in one mode instead of constantly loading and unloading different contexts.

**3. Automate the transitions.**

This is where the first four days connect.

If your marketing runs itself (Day 1), you don't need to switch into marketer mode.
If your tools talk to each other (Day 2), you don't need to manually move data.
If your support handles the common questions (Day 3), you don't need to check your inbox every 20 minutes.
If your content is batched and systemised (Day 4), you don't need to create something new every day.

Each leak you plug is one fewer hat you wear. One fewer switch. One fewer interruption.

That's why this leak ties them all together. Fix the other four, and this one starts fixing itself.
