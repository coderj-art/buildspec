# Day 3: The Support Leak

**Subject:** the same 10 questions. every week.
**Sub-text:** and you're still answering them yourself

---

Yesterday we talked about the operations leak. Duct-taping tools together and being the human glue holding it all in place.

Today's one is going to sting a little. Because you already know you're doing this. You just haven't stopped to think about what it's costing you.

Think about your last week. How many times did someone ask you a question you've already answered before?

"How does pricing work?"
"What's included?"
"How do I get started?"
"Can you send me that link again?"

Be honest. It's probably somewhere between 5 and 20 times.

That's the support leak.

**The Mistake**

Personally answering the same 10 questions every week.

Every support ticket answered by you. Every DM replied to by you. Every email explained by you.

I spoke to a coach recently who tracked her time for a week. She was spending 60% of her working hours on tasks that weren't coaching. Email replies. Sending resources. Answering the same onboarding questions for every new client.

She wasn't running a coaching business. She was running a help desk that happened to also do coaching.

And the mad thing is, she was proud of her response time. "I reply within an hour." Which sounds great. Until you realise that fast responses to repetitive questions is just efficient time wasting.

**The Reason This Happens**

You think only you can answer these properly.

And at the start, that was true. You were the only one who knew the business. You were the only one who understood the nuance.

But that was 200 customers ago. By now, 80% of the questions you get have the same answer. The same link. The same explanation.

You've just never written them down somewhere a system can use them.

So every time someone asks, you type it out again. From memory. With slight variations. Taking 5 minutes each time when it should take zero.

The other reason this happens is ego. It feels good to be needed. It feels like good customer service. "I personally respond to every message."

But your customers don't care who answers. They care how fast they get the right answer. A system that responds in 10 seconds with the perfect answer beats you responding in an hour with a slightly different version of the same thing.

**How To Fix It**

**1. Track your questions for one week.**

Every time someone asks you something, write it down. Don't try to categorise it yet. Just collect them.

By Friday you'll have a list. And I guarantee 80% of that list is the same 8-10 questions repeated.

**2. Write your top 10 answers. Once.**

Take those 8-10 questions. Write the perfect answer for each one. Take your time. Make them clear, complete, and friendly.

Save them somewhere you can access them instantly. A Google Doc. A Notion page. A text file on your desktop. Doesn't matter where. Just get them out of your head and into a file.

**3. Automate the delivery.**

Start simple. Set up canned responses in your email. Create a FAQ page and start linking to it instead of typing answers.

Then level up. A chatbot on your website that handles the top 10. Auto-replies in your DMs. A welcome sequence for new clients that answers everything before they ask.

The goal isn't to remove yourself from support entirely. The goal is to only handle the 20% of questions that actually need a human. The ones that are unique. Complex. Sensitive.

Everything else should answer itself.
