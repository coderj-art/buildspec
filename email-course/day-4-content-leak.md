# Day 4: The Content Leak

**Subject:** $340/month on AI tools. still stuck.
**Sub-text:** buying tools and using them are two completely different things

---

Yesterday we talked about the support leak. Answering the same questions over and over when a system could do it for you.

Today's leak is the one I hear about the most. Because almost everyone I talk to is experiencing it right now.

You've bought the tools. ChatGPT. Maybe Claude. Maybe Jasper or Copy.ai. Maybe a few others.

You're paying $50 here. $30 there. $20 for this one. $40 for that one.

And you're still spending hours every day on content.

I heard from someone recently who added up their AI subscriptions. $340 a month. And she was still doing 6 hours of content work every single day.

That's the content leak.

**The Mistake**

Spending money on AI tools without building a system around them.

Here's what most people do. They open ChatGPT. Type "write me a LinkedIn post about X." Get something back. Tweak it for 20 minutes. Post it. Close the tab.

Then tomorrow they do the same thing. From scratch. No template. No process. No system.

They're using AI in short, disconnected bursts. Like borrowing a friend's car every time you need to go somewhere instead of just learning to drive.

The tool works. The approach doesn't.

**The Reason This Happens**

Nobody teaches you how to build the system. They teach you the tool.

Every YouTube video. Every Twitter thread. Every course. They show you how to use ChatGPT. How to write a prompt. How to get better output.

But nobody shows you how to connect it to your actual workflow. How to go from "I need 5 pieces of content this week" to "my system produces 5 pieces of content this week and I spend 45 minutes on it."

So you end up with powerful tools and no workflow. Like buying a commercial kitchen and still cooking one meal at a time.

The gap isn't the tool. It's the system around the tool. And until you build that system, you're just paying for expensive software that makes your manual process slightly faster.

**How To Fix It**

**1. Pick one content format.**

Not four. One. The one that works best for your business.

If email drives your revenue, focus on email. If LinkedIn brings you clients, focus on LinkedIn. If YouTube is your thing, focus on YouTube.

You can repurpose later. But right now, you need one format with one system.

**2. Build a repeatable workflow.**

Map out every step in your content process. For most people it looks something like this:

- Come up with an idea
- Research or outline it
- Write the first draft
- Edit it
- Format it
- Schedule or publish it

Now look at that list. Which steps can AI handle? For most people, the answer is: ideas, first drafts, and formatting. That's more than half the work.

Set this up once. Save your prompts. Save your templates. Save your formatting rules. Next time, you just feed in the topic and the system does the rest.

**3. Batch it.**

Stop creating content daily. Create it weekly.

Sit down once a week. Run your system. Produce all 5 pieces in one session. Schedule them. Done.

Batching works because you stay in one mode. You're not switching between "think of an idea" and "reply to that email" and "write a caption" all day. You do content once, and then you're free.

When I switched to batching, my content time went from about 2 hours a day to about 90 minutes a week. Same output. Less than a quarter of the time.

The tools didn't change. The system did.
